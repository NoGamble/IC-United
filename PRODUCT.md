# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Students at the IC (Integrated Circuit) College who are teammates, classmates, or curious peers. They browse on laptops and phones, expect fluid modern web experiences, and have zero patience for corporate or administrative-feeling interfaces. They come to feel proud of the team, discover teammates, and relive match moments.

## Product Purpose

A static showcase site for IC United FC, the football club of the Integrated Circuit College. The site documents the team's identity, honors, players, and memories. Success means every student who lands on it feels the team is credible, stylish, and worth following.

## Brand Personality

Fashion-forward, composed, cinematic. Three words: **cool, grounded, alive**.

The tone is confident without being loud. It carries the weight of real team identity, not a school project. English for display, headings, and navigation; Chinese only where necessary (player cards, labels that require it).

## Anti-references

- Admin dashboards and management software: fixed left sidebars, tabbed control panels, boxy card grids, rigid header/content/footer stacks.
- Generic school sports sites: clipart trophies, Comic Sans energy, centered tables with borders, low-contrast color schemes.
- Overproduced SaaS landing pages: hero-metric layouts, neon-on-dark gradients, glassmorphism stacked on glassmorphism, glowing particle fields.
- Anything that reads immediately as "AI made this": identical-height feature cards in rows of three, em-dash-heavy body copy, purple gradient CTAs.

## Design Principles

1. **Typography carries weight.** Large display type is the primary visual tool, not decoration. Let it breathe and dominate.
2. **Layout is organic, not administrative.** Avoid uniform grids. Use scale contrast, offset placement, and whitespace to create visual rhythm.
3. **Dark and composed.** Deep navy-to-black surface. Color is used sparingly and with intent, never as decoration.
4. **Interaction earns its place.** Every animation has a reason. No hover effects that exist only to prove the page is interactive.
5. **English face, Chinese soul.** Interface language is English for all structural and decorative text. Chinese appears only where content demands it (player names, bios, labels).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on all text. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for scroll and entrance animations. Semantic HTML first.
